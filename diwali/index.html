Diwali

Diwali, also known as Deepavali, is one of the most widely celebrated festivals in India and among Hindus worldwide. This festival of lights symbolizes the triumph of light over darkness and good over evil. Diwali typically spans five days and is marked by the illumination of homes and public spaces with oil lamps and colorful candles. Families come together to exchange gifts, share festive meals, and offer prayers to various deities, including Goddess Lakshmi, the goddess of wealth and prosperity. The bursting of fireworks and the creation of intricate rangoli patterns add to the vibrant atmosphere. Diwali is a time for reflection, spiritual renewal, and the strengthening of bonds with loved ones, emphasizing the values of unity, compassion, and the victory of righteousness.
