Onam

Onam is a vibrant and culturally significant festival celebrated primarily in the southern Indian state of Kerala. It is a harvest festival that commemorates the return of the mythical King Mahabali, symbolizing prosperity and abundance. The festival typically spans ten days and is marked by elaborate floral decorations called "Pookalam," traditional dance forms like Kathakali and Pulikali, and a grand feast known as the "Onam Sadya," featuring a variety of vegetarian dishes served on a banana leaf. People also engage in boat races, music, and cultural performances during this time. Onam holds deep cultural and religious significance for the people of Kerala, fostering a sense of unity and community celebration.
